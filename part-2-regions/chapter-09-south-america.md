# Chapter 7: South America - The Andean Chain

## The World's Longest Volcanic Arc

The Andes Mountains form the world's longest continental mountain range—7,000 km from Venezuela to the southern tip of Chile. This entire chain was built by the subduction of the Nazca and Antarctic Plates beneath the South American Plate, creating approximately **152 active volcanoes** and some of the highest volcanic peaks on Earth.

## The High Volcanoes: Where Fire Meets Sky

The Andes host the world's highest active volcanoes, with over 20 peaks exceeding 6,000 meters. At these elevations, volcanic summits pierce the tropopause, erupting into the stratosphere while wrapped in glaciers and permanent snow.

### The Highest Active Volcanoes on Earth

1. **Ojos del Salado** (Chile-Argentina): 6,893 m - Highest active volcano
2. **Llullaillaco** (Chile-Argentina): 6,739 m
3. **Tipas** (Argentina): 6,658 m
4. **Nevado Sajama** (Bolivia): 6,542 m

These volcanoes exist in one of Earth's harshest environments—extreme altitude, thin air, bitter cold, and intense solar radiation.

## Chile: The Volcanic Giant

**71 active volcanoes** • **World's third most volcanic country**

Chile's length (4,300 km) encompasses extraordinary volcanic diversity, from the Atacama Desert's high-altitude volcanoes to Patagonia's ice-draped peaks.

### Notable Chilean Volcanoes

#### Ojos del Salado
**Location**: Atacama Desert (Chile-Argentina border)
**Elevation**: 6,893 m
**Status**: Active (last eruption ~1,300 years ago, but active fumaroles)

The world's highest volcano (and second-highest peak in the Americas):
- Contains the world's highest lake (6,390 m)
- Extremely arid environment (Atacama is Earth's driest desert)
- **Summit crater** has fumaroles and sulfur deposits
- **Climbing**: Technical high-altitude mountaineering, 4WD approach

#### Villarrica
**Location**: Chilean Lake District
**Elevation**: 2,847 m
**Status**: One of Chile's most active volcanoes

- Contains active lava lake in summit crater
- Frequent strombolian eruptions
- **Major ski resort** on its slopes
- **Night skiing** with views of glowing crater
- Summit hikes popular (with guide)
- Tourist town of Pucón sits at its base

#### Calbuco
**Location**: Southern Chile
**Elevation**: 2,003 m
**Status**: Active (major eruption 2015)

- The 2015 eruption was a surprise—no warning
- Spectacular photos/videos of eruption plume
- Evacuated 6,500 people

#### Llaima
**Location**: Araucanía Region
**Elevation**: 3,125 m
**Status**: One of Chile's most active volcanoes

- Erupts every few years
- Located in Conguillio National Park
- Araucaria (monkey puzzle) forests on slopes

#### Nevados de Chillán
**Location**: Central Chile
**Elevation**: 3,212 m
**Status**: Active volcanic complex

- **Major ski resort** with thermal hot springs
- Geothermal activity: fumaroles, hot springs
- Some of Chile's best skiing combined with volcanic scenery

#### Osorno
**Location**: Chilean Lake District
**Elevation**: 2,652 m
**Status**: Active (last erupted 1869)

- Nearly perfect cone (South America's "Mount Fuji")
- **Ski resort** on northern slopes
- Visible from Puerto Varas resort town
- Climbed by thousands annually

#### Puyehue-Cordón Caulle
**Location**: Chilean Lake District
**Elevation**: 2,236 m (Puyehue)
**Status**: Active

- 2011 eruption from Cordón Caulle fissure
- Ash cloud disrupted global air travel
- Geothermal area with hot springs
- Long-duration eruption (2011-2012)

#### Lonquimay
**Location**: Araucanía Region
**Elevation**: 2,865 m
**Status**: Active

- 1988-1990 eruption created new vent (Navidad cone)
- Ski resort on slopes
- Lava tunnels and caves

#### Antuco
**Location**: Biobío Region
**Elevation**: 2,979 m
**Status**: Active

- Frequent historical eruptions
- Ski resort
- Military training disaster in 2005 (avalanche)

#### Llullaillaco
**Location**: Chile-Argentina border (Atacama)
**Elevation**: 6,739 m
**Status**: Active (last eruption 1877)

- Second-highest active volcano
- Archaeological site: Three Inca child mummies found at 6,739 m (best-preserved mummies ever)
- Extreme altitude climbing
- World's highest archaeological site

#### Láscar
**Location**: Atacama Desert
**Elevation**: 5,592 m
**Status**: Chile's most active volcano

- Frequent eruptions
- Large lava dome
- 1993 eruption visible from space
- Desert volcano with active fumaroles

#### Guallatiri
**Location**: Northern Chile
**Elevation**: 6,071 m
**Status**: Active

- One of world's highest active volcanoes
- Persistent fumarolic activity
- Remote high-altitude desert location

#### Cerro Hudson
**Location**: Southern Chile (Patagonia)
**Elevation**: 1,905 m
**Status**: Active

- 1991 eruption was Chile's second-largest 20th-century eruption (VEI 5+)
- Ice-covered stratovolcano
- Remote location

## Ecuador: Concentrated Volcanic Power

**21 active volcanoes** in a small country • **Avenue of the Volcanoes**

Ecuador packs remarkable volcanic density into its small area. The "Avenue of the Volcanoes" (as named by Alexander von Humboldt) features parallel rows of volcanic peaks running through the Andes.

### Notable Ecuadorian Volcanoes

#### Cotopaxi
**Location**: Central Ecuador, 50 km S of Quito
**Elevation**: 5,897 m
**Status**: Active (last major eruption 1877)

One of the world's highest active volcanoes and most beautiful:
- Nearly **perfect snow-capped cone**
- Glacier-covered despite being near the equator
- Massive glaciers pose lahar risk to valleys below
- **Popular climb**: 5,000+ attempts annually
- Technical glacier climb but achievable for experienced mountaineers

#### Chimborazo
**Location**: Central Ecuador
**Elevation**: 6,263 m (Ecuador's highest peak)
**Status**: Dormant (last eruption ~550 CE)

The point on Earth's surface farthest from the center:
- Due to Earth's equatorial bulge, Chimborazo's summit is **6,384 km** from Earth's center
- (Everest is ~6,382 km from center)
- **The farthest point from Earth's core you can stand**
- Heavily glaciated
- **Climbing**: High-altitude mountaineering

#### Tungurahua ("Throat of Fire")
**Location**: Central Ecuador
**Elevation**: 5,023 m
**Status**: Active—continuous eruptions since 1999

- Erupts frequently with ash and lava
- Evacuations of Baños (tourist town) have occurred
- Spectacular nighttime displays of glowing lava

#### Quilotoa
**Location**: Central Ecuador
**Elevation**: 3,914 m (crater rim)
**Status**: Dormant (last eruption ~800 years ago)

- Stunning **crater lake** filled with blue-green water
- 3 km wide caldera
- **Popular hike**: Rim trail and descent to lake
- Accessible day trip from Quito

#### Cayambe
**Location**: Northern Ecuador
**Elevation**: 5,790 m
**Status**: Active (last eruption ~1785)

- Third-highest peak in Ecuador
- Highest point on Earth directly on the Equator (0° latitude)
- Glaciated summit
- Technical ice climbing required

#### Reventador
**Location**: Eastern Ecuador (Amazon side)
**Elevation**: 3,562 m
**Status**: One of Ecuador's most active volcanoes

- Nearly constant activity
- 2002 eruption was major (VEI 4)
- Remote jungle location
- Spectacular lava flows and explosions

#### Sangay
**Location**: Eastern Ecuador
**Elevation**: 5,230 m
**Status**: Extremely active—one of world's most active

- Erupting almost continuously since 1934
- Very remote location
- UNESCO World Heritage Site (Sangay National Park)
- Difficult access through jungle

#### Antisana
**Location**: Northern Ecuador
**Elevation**: 5,704 m
**Status**: Active

- Fourth-highest peak in Ecuador
- Heavily glaciated
- Rare wildlife (condors, spectacled bears)
- Technical climbing

#### Altar (Capac Urcu)
**Location**: Central Ecuador
**Elevation**: 5,319 m
**Status**: Extinct but geologically young

- Collapsed volcano with horseshoe-shaped caldera
- Glacial lagoon in caldera
- Spectacular trekking destination
- Once may have been Ecuador's highest peak

### Galápagos Islands (Ecuador)

**~30 volcanoes** across the archipelago

The Galápagos sit over a volcanic hot spot, similar to Hawaii:

#### Fernandina
**Elevation**: 1,476 m
**Status**: One of the world's most active volcanoes

- Eruptions in 2009, 2017, 2018, 2020
- Pristine ecosystem - no introduced species
- Large summit caldera
- Classic shield volcano

#### Wolf Volcano (Isabela Island)
**Elevation**: 1,707 m (highest point in Galápagos)
**Status**: Active (erupted 2015, 2022)

- Large shield volcano
- Pink iguanas live on slopes (unique species)
- Caldera at summit

#### Sierra Negra (Isabela Island)
**Elevation**: 1,124 m
**Status**: Active

- Second-largest volcanic caldera in world (10 × 9 km)
- 2018 eruption was major
- Accessible hiking to caldera rim
- Popular tourist destination

#### Alcedo, Darwin, Ecuador volcanoes (Isabela)
- Isabela formed by six shield volcanoes merging
- Each with distinct ecosystems

**Unique**: Galápagos volcanoes shaped the evolution Charles Darwin observed. Marine iguanas, giant tortoises, and finches evolved on isolated volcanic islands.

### Additional Ecuadorian Volcanoes

**Mainland**: Imbabura, Cuicocha, Guagua Pichincha (overlooks Quito), Rucu Pichincha, Atacazo, Corazón, Iliniza, Chalupas, Sumaco

## Colombia: The Northern Andes

**~15 active volcanoes**

### Notable Colombian Volcanoes

#### Nevado del Ruiz
**Location**: Central Colombia
**Elevation**: 5,321 m
**Status**: Active

Site of the deadliest volcanic disaster in recent history:
- **November 13, 1985**: Small eruption melted glacial ice
- Generated massive lahars
- Buried the town of **Armero** 74 km away
- Killed **23,000+ people**
- Most deaths occurred at night
- Tragedy was preventable—warning signs ignored

The catastrophe transformed volcanic hazard monitoring and response worldwide.

## Why the Andes Are Exceptional

### Extreme Altitude
The Andes host the world's highest volcanoes—erupting at elevations where the atmosphere is 40-50% thinner than sea level. This affects:
- **Eruption dynamics**: Lower atmospheric pressure changes how magma erupts
- **Hazards**: Glaciers + eruptions = massive lahar potential
- **Access**: Extreme altitude makes these volcanoes serious mountaineering challenges

### Glaciers and Ice
Despite being in the tropics, many Andean volcanoes support permanent glaciers:
- **Cotopaxi**: One of few equatorial glaciers
- **Calbuco, Villarrica**: Patagonian ice-clad volcanoes
- **Climate change**: Andean glaciers are rapidly retreating

### Cultural Significance
Andean volcanoes have been sacred for millennia:
- **Inca ritual sites**: Found on many summits (including 6,000+ m peaks)
- **Human sacrifices**: High-altitude volcanoes contain the best-preserved Inca mummies
- **Modern spirituality**: Volcanoes remain culturally important

### Adventure Capital
The Andes offer some of the world's best volcanic adventures:
- **High-altitude mountaineering**: Cotopaxi, Chimborazo, Ojos del Salado
- **Volcano skiing**: Villarrica, Osorno, Nevados de Chillán
- **Accessible craters**: Quilotoa, crater lakes
- **Volcanic hot springs**: Hundreds of thermal springs throughout the range

## The Living Range

The Andes continue to build. The Nazca Plate subducts beneath South America at 7-8 cm per year, ensuring volcanic activity will continue for millions of years. Eruptions reshape the landscape, glaciers carve the peaks, and earthquakes reminder residents that this magnificent range is very much alive.

From the Atacama's alien high-altitude volcanoes to Patagonia's ice-draped peaks, the Andes showcase volcanic processes at their most extreme—and most beautiful.
